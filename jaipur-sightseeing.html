15 Iconic Pink City Landmarks

Every single landmark below is fully accessible via our local sightseeing cabs. Tell our driver which spots you want to prioritize, and we will build a seamless custom route.

01. UNESCO Hill Citadel

Amber Fort & Palace

Perched on the rugged Aravalli hills overlooking Maota Lake. Features the dazzling Sheesh Mahal (Palace of Mirrors), Sukh Niwas, and magnificent Rajput-Mughal gates.

02. Sunset Crown

Nahargarh Fort

Built in 1734 on the ridge of the Aravalli Hills as a strong defense ring. Offers the most breathtaking panoramic views of the sprawling Pink City at sunset and night.

03. Victory Fortress

Jaigarh Fort

Connected to Amber Fort via subterranean passages. Home to the Jaivana Cannon—once the world's largest cannon on wheels—and ancient royal cannon foundries.

04. Architectural Icon

Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds)

Constructed in 1799 in the shape of Lord Krishna's crown. Its 953 intricate pink sandstone jharokha lattice windows allowed royal ladies to observe street festivals.

05. Royal Residence

City Palace Complex

Located in the exact heart of the old walled city. A majestic blend of Rajput, Mughal, and European architecture featuring Mubarak Mahal, Pritam Niwas Chowk, and royal armories.

06. UNESCO Observatory

Jantar Mantar

Built in 1734 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II. A collection of 19 architectural astronomical instruments including Brihat Samrat Yantra, the world's largest stone sundial.

07. Floating Palace

Jal Mahal (Water Palace)

Floating serenely amidst Man Sagar Lake against the backdrop of the Nahargarh hills. Four floors remain submerged underwater while the top terrace shines in golden light.

08. Indo-Saracenic Marvel

Albert Hall Museum

The oldest museum in Rajasthan, located in Ram Niwas Garden. Famous for its exquisite collection of miniature paintings, royal carpets, antique armory, and an Egyptian mummy.

09. Marble Sanctuary

Birla Mandir (Laxmi Narayan Temple)

Constructed entirely out of pure white Makrana marble beneath Moti Dungri Hill. Dedicated to Lord Vishnu and Goddess Laxmi, glowing brilliantly at sunset.

10. Ancient Pilgrimage

Galtaji (Monkey Temple)

An ancient Hindu pilgrimage site nestled within a mountain pass. Famous for its natural freshwater springs (kunds), temples built into rock faces, and friendly rhesus macaques.

11. Instagram Sensation

Patrika Gate (Jawahar Circle)

A massive rainbow-colored gateway illustrating the rich history, folk dances, and architecture of every region in Rajasthan through hand-painted frescoes on nine pillars.

12. Stepwell Wonder

Panna Meena Ka Kund

A 16th-century symmetrical stepwell located near Amber Fort. Designed with crisscrossing geometrical staircases that create mesmerizing visual illusions.

13. Royal Garden Sanctuary

Sisodia Rani Ka Bagh

Built by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II for his Udaipur queen. Features multi-tiered Mughal-Rajput gardens, water fountains, pavilions, and murals depicting Lord Krishna's love stories.

14. Cultural Village

Chokhi Dhani Ethnic Resort

A 5-star ethnic village resort celebrating Rajasthani village culture. Enjoy live Kalbelia folk dances, puppet shows, acrobatics, and sumptuous traditional dinner thalis.

15. Royal Crematorium

Royal Gaitore Cenotaphs

Set beneath the Nahargarh hills, this serene royal crematorium features beautifully carved white marble chhatris (cenotaphs) dedicated to Jaipur's past Kachwaha rulers.

21 Revered Temples & Shrines of Rajasthan

Explore the divine history of each sacred destination covered by our cab packages. We provide direct point-to-point transfers from airport, railway stations, or residences.

01. Sikar District

Khatu Shyam Ji Mandir

Dedicated to Barbarika, grandson of Bhima from Mahabharata, worshipped as Shyam Baba—the savior of the defeated (Haare Ka Sahara). Located ~80 km from Jaipur.

02. Churu District

Salasar Balaji Temple

One of the most famous shrines of Lord Hanuman in India, unique for depicting Balaji with round eyes and a mustache. Attracts millions during Chaitra and Ashwin Purnima.

03. Dausa District

Shri Mehandipur Balaji Mandir

Revered nationwide for exorcizing negative energies and curing distress. Houses three deities: Lord Balaji, Bhairav Baba (Kotwal Kaptan), and Pretraj Sarkar.

04. Ajmer City

Ajmer Sharif Dargah

The Sufi shrine of revered saint Garib Nawaz (Khwaja Moinuddin Chishti). A symbol of communal harmony visited by people of all faiths seeking blessings and peace.

05. Holy Pushkar

Jagatpita Brahma Mandir

The most prominent temple in the world dedicated to Lord Brahma the Creator, standing near the sacred Pushkar Lake surrounded by 52 bathing ghats.

06. Jaipur City Palace

Govind Dev Ji Temple

The principal deity of Jaipur's royal family. The idol of Lord Krishna was brought from Vrindavan by Raja Sawai Jai Singh II and exactly resembles Krishna's divine form.

07. Jaipur City

Birla Mandir (Laxmi Narayan)

Carved completely out of shimmering white Makrana marble below Moti Dungri Hill. Features mythological carvings alongside portraits of global philosophers.

08. Jaipur City

Moti Dungri Ganesh Mandir

Jaipur's most popular Ganesh shrine. Thousands of devotees visit every Wednesday and on Ganesh Chaturthi to seek auspicious beginnings and obstacle removal.

09. Jaipur Hills

Galtaji (Tirth Raj)

An ancient Hindu monastic complex situated within a mountain crevice. Renowned for its natural sacred water tanks (Galta Kund) where pilgrims take holy dips.

10. Jaipur Highway

Khole Ke Hanuman Ji

Perched inside the valley of Laxman Dungri hills on the Delhi-Jaipur bypass. Known for its massive prasadi feasts (Dal Baati Churma) and serene hilltop atmosphere.

11. Chitrakoot Jaipur

Akshardham Mandir (BAPS)

Dedicated to Bhagwan Swaminarayan. Celebrated for its intricate stone carvings, peaceful Vedic garden, and enlightening cultural exhibitions.

12. Nahargarh Hilltop

Garh Ganesh Temple

Ancient temple where Lord Ganesh is worshipped in the form of a young child without his trunk (Purushakriti). Built by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II prior to founding Jaipur.

13. Chandi Ki Taksal

Kale Hanuman Ji Temple

One of the very few temples in India where Lord Hanuman is depicted in black color. Located near Hawa Mahal in the old walled city of Jaipur.

14. Amber Fort Courtyard

Shila Devi Temple (Amber)

Housed inside Amber Fort, dedicated to Goddess Durga. The deity idol was brought by Maharaja Man Singh I from Jessore (now Bangladesh) in 1604.

15. Tonk District

Shri Diggi Kalyan Ji Mandir

An ancient shrine of Lord Vishnu located in Malpura, Tonk (~80 km from Jaipur). Known for curing chronic illnesses and granting inner tranquility.

16. Bikaner District

Karni Mata Mandir (Rat Temple)

World-famous as the 'Rat Temple' of Deshnok near Bikaner. Home to over 25,000 revered black rats (Kabbas) believed to be incarnations of Karni Mata's devotees.

17. Karauli District

Shri Mahavirji Jain Sanctuary

One of the most sacred Digambar Jain pilgrimage sites in India. Houses a miraculous ancient red sandstone idol of Bhagwan Mahavir inside a grand white marble temple.

18. Jhunjhunu City

Rani Sati Dadi Mandir

A massive marble temple complex in Jhunjhunu paying tribute to Rani Sati Dadi, revered as an incarnation of Goddess Durga for feminine courage and family devotion.

19. Sambhar Lake / Shekhawati

Shakambhari Mata Temple

Revered ancient temple dedicated to Goddess Shakambhari (the incarnation of Durga who nourished mankind with greens and herbs during drought), situated on scenic salt flats.

20. Sawai Madhopur

Trinetra Ganesh Ji Mandir

Located inside the historic Ranthambore Fort. The only temple where Lord Ganesh is enshrined with his three eyes along with his wives Riddhi-Siddhi and sons Shubh-Labh.

21. Dausa District

Harshad Mata Temple (Abhaneri)

A 9th-century temple dedicated to Goddess Harshad Mata (the Goddess of Joy and Happiness), situated right beside the magnificent Chand Baori architectural stepwell.

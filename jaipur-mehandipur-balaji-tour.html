Route Overview & Highlights

Heading east from Jaipur toward Dausa district, this 8-hour excursion is perfect for travelers wishing to experience deep Rajasthani faith alongside medieval engineering. The route is entirely on the smooth 4-lane Jaipur-Agra National Highway 21, making travel effortless for families with children or elderly parents.

Mehandipur Balaji Temple

World-famous Hanuman shrine dedicated to overcoming negative energies and wish fulfillment.

Chand Baori (Abhaneri)

1,200-year-old architectural masterpiece featuring 3,500 perfectly geometric stairs descending 13 levels.

Harshat Mata Temple

Intricately carved 9th-century temple dedicated to the Goddess of Joy and Happiness.

03:00 PM — 04:30 PM (Return)

Frequently Asked Questions

Are there any specific rules to follow inside Mehandipur Balaji Temple?

According to traditional custom, devotees visiting Mehandipur Balaji do not eat Prasad inside or bring Prasad/water back home from the temple. Our knowledgeable chauffeur will brief you on all local customs during the drive so you can have a peaceful, positive Darshan.

Is Chand Baori stepwell open every day?

Yes, Chand Baori in Abhaneri is managed by the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI) and is open daily from sunrise to sunset. Photography is allowed throughout the complex.

Can we combine this tour with a one-way drop to Agra or Delhi?

Yes! Since Mehandipur Balaji and Abhaneri lie on the Jaipur-Agra highway, many of our guests book this itinerary as a sightseeing stopover while traveling from Jaipur to Agra (Taj Mahal) or Delhi. Contact our manager on WhatsApp for a custom one-way transfer quote.

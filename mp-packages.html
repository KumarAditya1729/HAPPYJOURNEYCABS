14 Iconic Shrines, UNESCO Wonders & Tiger Reserves

Explore every destination across Madhya Pradesh covered by our cab fleet. We offer point transfers and multi-day sightseeing from Jaipur, Delhi, Indore, and Bhopal.

01. Sacred Jyotirlinga Shrine

Ujjain (Mahakaleshwar & Bhasma Aarti)

The holy city on the banks of River Kshipra. Renowned for the south-facing Mahakaleshwar Jyotirlinga, grand Mahakal Lok Corridor, Kal Bhairav temple, and Harsiddhi Mandir.

02. Om-Shaped Narmada Island

Omkareshwar & Mamleshwar Jyotirlinga

Situated on Mandhata Island in the holy Narmada River, naturally shaped like the sacred symbol 'Om'. Home to both Omkareshwar and Mamleshwar Jyotirlinga shrines.

03. Cleanest City & Food Capital

Indore (Rajwada & Sarafa Bazar)

India's cleanest city known for rich Holkar dynastic heritage and culinary heaven. Explore the 7-story Holkar Rajwada Palace, Lal Bagh Palace, and late-night Sarafa street food.

04. UNESCO World Heritage Site

Khajuraho Group of Temples

Built by the Chandela dynasty between 950 and 1050 AD. World-famous for nagara-style architectural mastery and exquisite sculptural carvings, highlighted by Kandariya Mahadev.

05. Medieval Betwa River Town

Orchha (Ram Raja & Jehangir Mahal)

A frozen-in-time Bundela royal capital along the boulder-strewn Betwa River. The only temple where Lord Ram is worshipped as a King with royal police gun salutes daily.

06. City of Lakes

Bhopal (Upper Lake & Taj-ul-Masajid)

The state capital divided by twin artificial lakes. Home to India's largest mosque Taj-ul-Masajid, scenic Upper Lake boat cruises, Van Vihar National Park, and Bharat Bhawan.

07. UNESCO Buddhist Wonder

Sanchi Stupa Complex

Commissioned by Mauryan Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE (~46 km from Bhopal). Features the Great Stupa with four intricately carved gateways (Toranas) depicting Buddha's life.

08. UNESCO Prehistoric Art

Bhimbetka Rock Shelters

Located inside Ratapani Wildlife Sanctuary (~45 km from Bhopal). Showcases over 700 rock shelters with Paleolithic cave paintings dating back more than 30,000 years.

09. City of Joy Fortresses

Mandu (Jahaz Mahal & Roopmati Pavilion)

A massive hilltop fortress city in the Vindhya range (~95 km from Indore). Famous for Afghan architectural gems: floating Jahaz Mahal, Hindola Mahal, and romantic Roopmati Pavilion.

10. Ahilya Bai Holkar Capital

Maheshwar Ghats & Fort

Set on the banks of Narmada River (~90 km from Indore). The glorious capital of Queen Ahilyabai Holkar, famous for stunning stone ghats and delicate Maheshwari silk weaving looms.

11. Queen of Satpura

Pachmarhi Hill Station

The only hill station in Madhya Pradesh, perched at 3,555 ft in the Satpura range. Known for cascading Bee Fall, Rajat Prapat, Pandav Caves, and dramatic sunset vistas from Dhupgarh.

12. Royal Bengal Tiger Habitat

Kanha Tiger Reserve

One of India's largest and most pristine tiger reserves, featuring vast sal and bamboo forests. The only place on earth where the rare Hardground Barasingha (swamp deer) was saved.

13. Highest Tiger Density Reserve

Bandhavgarh National Park

Nestled among the Vindhya hills around an ancient cliff-top fort. Renowned globally for having the highest density of Royal Bengal tigers in India across Tala and Magdhi safari zones.

14. Kipling's Jungle Book Forest

Pench National Park

Spanning the southern slopes of Satpura range along the Pench River. The literal setting that inspired Rudyard Kipling's classic 'The Jungle Book', rich in leopards, tigers, and dholes.

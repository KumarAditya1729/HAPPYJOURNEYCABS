✦ Complete Mountain Directory ✦

17 Iconic Hill Stations & Valleys of Himachal

Explore our directory of every Himalayan destination covered by Happy Journey Cabs. We provide safe, transparent point-to-point transfers and sightseeing cab packages.

01. Capital Hill Station

Shimla ("Queen of Hill Stations")

The historic summer capital of British India. Famous for the bustling pedestrian Mall Road, neo-gothic Christ Church on The Ridge, and panoramic Jakhoo Temple ropeway.

02. Snow & Adventure Hub

Kufri

Located ~16 km uphill from Shimla at 8,600 ft. Famous for winter skiing, Himalayan Nature Park, horse riding up to Mahasu Peak, and breathtaking cedar forest viewpoints.

03. Valley of the Gods

Manali & Old Manali

Nestled in the Beas River Valley at 6,726 ft. Renowned for the 16th-century wooden Hadimba Temple, Tibetan monasteries, Vashisht hot springs, and vibrant Old Manali cafes.

04. Adventure Playground

Solang Valley & Atal Tunnel

The ultimate adventure hub near Manali. Enjoy thrilling paragliding flights, zorbing, snow quad-biking, and drive through the 9.02-km Atal Tunnel into Lahaul's Sissu waterfall.

05. River & Shawl Valley

Kullu Valley

An open valley stretching along the Beas River between Mandi and Manali. Celebrated for white-water river rafting, apple orchards, and authentic Kullu Pashmina shawl factories.

06. Parvati Valley Heart

Kasol ("Little Israel")

A picturesque village situated along the rushing Parvati River. Popular for its bohemian riverside cafes, pine forests, Israeli cuisine, and base for Kheerganga and Malana treks.

07. Sacred Hot Springs

Manikaran Sahib

A revered pilgrimage center for both Sikhs and Hindus in Parvati Valley. Famous for boiling sulphur hot springs where langar rice is cooked directly in natural geothermal waters.

08. Alpine Trekking Base

Tosh & Kheerganga

Perched at the far end of Parvati Valley. Offers traditional wooden Himachali homestays, dramatic views of Kutla glacier, and the starting point for the natural hot bath Kheerganga trek.

09. Offbeat Eco Retreat

Jibhi & Tirthan Valley

An unexplored eco-tourism haven inside the Great Himalayan National Park zone. Famous for Victorian stone cottages, Jibhi waterfalls, trout fishing, and Jalori Pass.

10. Colonial Pine Sanctuary

Dalhousie

Spread across five scenic hills in Chamba district. Celebrated for its Scottish-era St. John's Church, Subhash Baoli, Panchpula waterfalls, and panoramic views of Pir Panjal range.

11. Mini Switzerland of India

Khajjiar Meadow & Lake

Officially christened as 'Mini Switzerland' by the Swiss ambassador in 1992. A saucer-shaped emerald plateau surrounded by dense deodar forests featuring a floating island lake.

12. Dhauladhar Gateway

Dharamshala & Kangra Valley

Set against the dramatic snow-capped Dhauladhar mountains. Home to the world-famous HPCA Cricket Stadium, Kangra Art Museum, War Memorial, and ancient tea gardens.

13. Little Lhasa

McLeodganj (Dalai Lama Abode)

The spiritual residence of His Holiness the Dalai Lama. Famous for Tsuglagkhang Complex, Bhagsu Nag waterfall & Shiva temple, Tibetan crafts market, and Triund peak trek.

14. World Paragliding Capital

Bir Billing Paragliding Hub

Recognized as the 2nd highest and finest paragliding takeoff site in the world at 8,000 ft (Billing), landing in peaceful Bir village known for the grand Chokling Monastery.

15. Trans-Himalayan Cold Desert

Spiti Valley (Kaza & Key)

A rugged cold desert valley separated from Tibet by high mountain ridges. Home to the iconic 1000-year-old Key Monastery, Dhankar Gompa, Langza Buddha statue, and Hikkim post office.

16. Crescent Moon Lake

Chandratal Lake (14,100 ft)

Known as the 'Lake of the Moon' due to its crescent shape. Nestled on the Samudra Tapu plateau in Lahaul-Spiti, offering crystal turquoise waters and starlit luxury camping.

17. High Altitude Glacier Pass

Rohtang Pass (13,058 ft)

The high mountain gateway connecting Manali to Lahaul and Spiti valleys. Offers year-round snow panoramas, sledge rides, and dramatic glacial terrain (Requires special NGT permit).
